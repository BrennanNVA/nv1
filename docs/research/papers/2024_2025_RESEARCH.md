# Latest Research Papers (2024-2025)

## Overview

This document summarizes key research papers from 2024-2025 relevant to Nova Aetus trading system.

## Key Papers

### 1. "Machine Learning the Performance of Hedge Fund" (2025)

**Key Findings:**
- Transformer-based Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) models show superior performance
- Multi-horizon forecasting improves signal quality
- Attention mechanisms capture regime-dependent patterns

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Supports multi-horizon model implementation
- Validates transformer architectures for financial time series

### 2. "Anomalies as New Hedge Fund Factors" (2025)

**Key Findings:**
- 9-factor model with anomaly factors outperforms traditional factors
- Anomaly factors capture market inefficiencies
- Factor rotation based on regime improves returns

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Supports regime-aware factor selection
- Validates anomaly detection approaches

### 3. "Revolutionizing Hedge Fund Risk Management with Deep Learning" (2024)

**Key Findings:**
- LSTM-based hedging strategies reduce portfolio risk
- Deep learning improves dynamic hedging
- Real-time risk adjustment based on market conditions

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Supports dynamic hedging strategies
- Validates deep learning for risk management

### 4. "A Comprehensive Review of Generative AI in Hedge Funds" (2025)

**Key Findings:**
- Generative AI shows 3-5% return improvement
- LLM-based sentiment analysis improves signal quality
- Multi-modal data fusion enhances predictions

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Validates Ollama/Local LLM approach for sentiment
- Supports multi-modal data integration

### 5. "Ensemble Methods in Quantitative Trading" (2024)

**Key Findings:**
- Stacking shows 3-5% higher returns vs single models
- Ridge meta-learner optimal for financial data
- Out-of-fold predictions critical for preventing leakage

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Validates ensemble stacking implementation
- Supports meta-learner approach

### 6. "Execution Cost Modeling in High-Frequency Trading" (2024)

**Key Findings:**
- Execution costs erode 20-40% of alpha
- Almgren-Chriss framework optimal for large orders
- VWAP/TWAP reduce market impact

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Validates execution cost modeling
- Supports optimal execution strategies

### 7. "Alpha Decay and Signal Half-Life in Financial Markets" (2024)

**Key Findings:**
- US markets: 5.6% annual alpha decay
- European markets: 9.9% annual alpha decay
- Signal half-life varies by market regime

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Validates IC decay monitoring
- Supports signal rotation strategies

### 8. "Observable vs Hidden Regime Switching" (2025)

**Key Findings:**
- Observable switching detects changes 2-3x faster
- Leading indicators (VIX, yield curve) improve regime detection
- Regime-aware models outperform static models

**Relevance to Nova Aetus:**
- Validates observable regime detection
- Supports correlation-based regime detection
